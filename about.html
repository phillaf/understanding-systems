About

Hi, I'm Phil. I studied computer science, and my career has taken me through software development, building systems, debugging problems, and trying to make sense of complex data.

Why This Blog?

This blog is an experiment in understanding—understanding how the world works, how systems connect, and where power and influence flow. I'm applying the same analytical mindset I learned in computer science to explore economics, corporate networks, financial systems, and the structures that shape our world.

I'm not an expert in these fields. This is exploratory work—testing ideas, implementing methodologies from research papers, connecting dots, and seeing if the patterns make sense.

Important disclaimer: I am not an economist, political scientist, or expert in the fields I explore here. This blog should not be viewed as a trustworthy source for professional analysis or advice. Everything here is exploratory, educational (for me), and should be taken with healthy skepticism.

If you're looking for authoritative analysis, please consult actual experts and peer-reviewed research.

About the Writing

The text on this blog has been written by AI under my supervision. I provide the direction, structure, and ideas, but the AI handles the heavy lifting of turning data analysis into readable prose.

What You'll Find Here

This blog explores systems—economic, political, corporate, and social. Topics include:

  • Economic indicators and patterns in market data
  • Corporate ownership networks and media influence
  • Power structures and institutional connections
  • Financial systems, banking, and investment mechanics
  • Technology infrastructure and data ecosystems
  • Testing whether published methodologies actually work
  • Building transparent, reproducible analysis pipelines

All analysis uses publicly available data and open methodologies. The goal is to make everything clear, reproducible, and open to scrutiny.
